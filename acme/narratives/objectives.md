# Information Security Objectives

This document records the information security objectives of {{.Name}} in accordance with ISO 27001:2022 Clause 6.2.

# Objectives

The following information security objectives have been established and approved by management:

a. *Protect the confidentiality of customer data.* Customer personal data and commercially sensitive information must be protected from unauthorised access or disclosure. Progress is measured by the number of confirmed data breach incidents per year (target: zero).

a. *Maintain the availability of critical systems.* Production systems must meet defined availability targets. Progress is measured by monthly uptime reporting against agreed service level objectives.

a. *Ensure timely response to security incidents.* All security incidents must be identified, assessed, and contained in accordance with the Security Incident Response Policy. Progress is measured by mean time to detection and mean time to containment.

a. *Reduce the organisation's attack surface.* Vulnerabilities in systems and applications must be identified and remediated within defined timeframes according to severity. Progress is measured by the proportion of high and critical vulnerabilities remediated within target timescales.

a. *Maintain compliance with applicable legal and regulatory requirements.* The organisation must identify and comply with all relevant legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations relating to information security. Progress is measured through annual compliance reviews and audit findings.

# Review

These objectives are reviewed annually by senior management as part of the ISMS management review process, or sooner if significant changes to the organisation's context or risk profile occur.
