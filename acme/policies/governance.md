# Purpose

This policy defines how {{.Name}} structures information security roles and responsibilities, maintains appropriate segregation of duties, and engages with relevant external authorities and interest groups.

# Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties who hold information security responsibilities within {{.Name}}.

# Segregation of Duties

{{.Name}} ensures that conflicting duties and areas of responsibility are separated to reduce opportunities for unauthorised or unintentional modification or misuse of the organisation's assets. Where segregation of duties is not achievable due to organisational size, compensating controls such as audit trails, monitoring, and management oversight are applied.

No individual shall have sole control over any critical business process from initiation to completion. Specific segregation requirements are documented in role definitions and enforced through access controls and system configurations.

# Contact with Authorities

{{.Name}} maintains documented contacts with relevant authorities including regulatory bodies, law enforcement agencies, and emergency services. These contacts are reviewed and updated at least annually. Designated personnel are responsible for initiating and maintaining these relationships. Contacts with authorities are invoked in the event of information security incidents that may require regulatory notification or law enforcement involvement.

# Contact with Special Interest Groups

{{.Name}} maintains membership in or contact with appropriate special interest groups, professional forums, and information-sharing communities relevant to information security. These relationships provide access to specialist information, early warning of threats, and current security advice. Participation in such groups is approved by management and relevant information gained is shared internally through appropriate channels.

# Responsibilities

The Information Security function is responsible for maintaining registers of authority contacts and special interest group memberships, and for ensuring segregation of duties requirements are reviewed when roles change.
