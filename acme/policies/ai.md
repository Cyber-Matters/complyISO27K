# Purpose and Scope

a. This policy defines the acceptable and secure use of artificial intelligence (AI) tools and services, including large language models, generative AI platforms, and AI-assisted development tools.

a. This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and other personnel who use AI tools in the course of their work for the organisation.

a. This policy applies to both organisation-provided AI tools and third-party AI services accessed for business purposes.

# Background

a. AI tools can significantly improve productivity and quality of work. However, their use also introduces risks including inadvertent disclosure of confidential information, introduction of inaccurate or biased outputs, intellectual property concerns, and security vulnerabilities in AI-generated code.

a. This policy establishes a framework for using AI tools responsibly while managing these risks.

# Policy

a. *Approved tools*

    i. Employees must only use AI tools that have been reviewed and approved by the Information Security team.

    i. A current list of approved AI tools is maintained by the Information Security team and made available to all employees.

    i. Requests to use new AI tools must be submitted to the Information Security team for evaluation prior to use.

a. *Data classification restrictions*

    i. Confidential data, including customer personal data, financial records, source code, and information classified as Restricted or Confidential under the Data Classification Policy, must not be entered into any AI tool unless the tool has been specifically approved for handling such data.

    i. Employees must treat outputs from AI tools as potentially inaccurate and must verify factual claims, code correctness, and legal or regulatory statements before relying on them.

a. *AI-generated code*

    i. All AI-generated code must be reviewed by a qualified developer before being committed to any codebase.

    i. AI-generated code must comply with the organisation's Secure Software Development Lifecycle Policy.

    i. Employees must not submit code to AI tools that contains credentials, API keys, or other secrets.

a. *Intellectual property*

    i. Employees must be aware that AI-generated content may incorporate third-party material subject to copyright. Legal advice should be sought before publishing or commercialising AI-generated content.

    i. Employees must not use AI tools to reverse-engineer, replicate, or circumvent the intellectual property of third parties.

a. *Accountability*

    i. Employees remain personally accountable for the accuracy, quality, and compliance of any work product produced with the assistance of AI tools.

    i. AI-generated content presented as the employee's own work must meet the same standards as work produced without AI assistance.
