# Purpose and Scope

a. This policy defines the acceptable use of information assets, systems, and technology resources owned or operated by the organisation.

a. This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, temporary workers, and other personnel who have been granted access to the organisation's information systems and data.

a. Violation of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or contract, and civil or criminal liability where applicable.

# Background

a. The organisation provides information systems and technology resources to support its business operations. These resources are provided for business purposes and must be used in a manner that is lawful, ethical, and consistent with the organisation's values and policies.

a. Inappropriate use of information assets exposes the organisation to risks including malware infection, compromise of confidential data, reputational damage, and legal liability.

# Policy

a. *Authorised use*

    i. Information systems and technology resources must be used primarily for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with job duties, consume significant resources, or violate any other provision of this policy.

    i. Users must only access systems, applications, and data for which they have been granted explicit authorisation.

    i. Users must not attempt to gain access to systems or data beyond their authorised level of access.

a. *Prohibited activities*

    i. The following activities are prohibited regardless of whether they are conducted on company equipment or personal devices:

        1. Accessing, transmitting, or storing material that is illegal, defamatory, harassing, or obscene.

        1. Using the organisation's systems to conduct personal business activities for financial gain.

        1. Installing unauthorised software, applications, or browser extensions on company equipment.

        1. Disabling or circumventing security controls, including endpoint protection, firewalls, or multi-factor authentication.

        1. Sharing credentials or allowing others to use one's account.

        1. Attempting to intercept, monitor, or alter network traffic without authorisation.

        1. Using the organisation's resources to conduct or support attacks against other systems.

a. *Social media and public communications*

    i. Employees must not disclose confidential information about the organisation, its customers, or its partners through social media or other public channels.

    i. Employees must make clear that personal opinions expressed online are their own and do not represent the organisation.

a. *Monitoring*

    i. The organisation reserves the right to monitor the use of its information systems and technology resources in accordance with applicable law and with appropriate notice to users.

a. *Reporting*

    i. Users must report suspected violations of this policy or any suspicious activity to the Information Security team promptly.
